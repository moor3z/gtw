Common Questions

End of tenancy garden clearance — FAQs

How quickly can you clear a garden between tenants?

Same-week slots are often available, and most gardens are cleared in a single day. Tell us the move-in date and we'll work to it — and if a deadline can't be met, we'll say so upfront rather than leave you guessing.

Can you quote from our checkout or inventory photos?

Yes — that's how most of our agent work starts. Send the photos from the checkout report or inspection and you'll get a fixed price, usually within one working day. No need to arrange access twice.

Do you need someone at the property?

No. We regularly collect keys from agents' offices or work via lockboxes and side-gate access. The property doesn't need to be attended, and keys come back the same day.

Do you provide evidence for deposit deductions?

Every clearance comes with dated before-and-after photos and an itemised invoice. How garden costs are handled with the deposit is between landlord, tenant and the deposit scheme — but you'll have a clear record of the condition and the work done.

Whose responsibility is an overgrown garden at the end of a tenancy?

It depends on the tenancy agreement — most expect the garden back in broadly the condition it was let, allowing for seasonal growth. We can't give legal advice, but whatever the agreement says, we can put the garden right quickly and document it properly.
