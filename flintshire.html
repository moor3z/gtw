What a Flintshire garden clearance covers

Every clearance is quoted as a single fixed price. Here's what that price includes as standard — and what to mention up front so the quote is right first time.

Included in every clearance

  • Long grass, weeds and nettles cut right down — however far gone
  • Brambles and years of overgrowth cleared back
  • Hedges and shrubs cut back or removed, as agreed in the quote
  • All green waste loaded into the van and removed the same day
  • Legal disposal at a licensed waste site, as a registered waste carrier
  • Paths swept, gates closed, and before-and-after photos sent

Mention these when you ask for a quote

  • Large or tall trees — some jobs need a qualified tree surgeon instead
  • Soil, rubble, fencing, sheds or other non-green waste to be taken
  • Suspected Japanese knotweed (which legally requires specialist disposal) or running bamboo — both need a different approach to normal overgrowth
  • Anything you want protected — established plants, borders, features
  • Tight or shared access for loading (rear alleys, no parking, steep drives)

None of these are automatically a "no" — they just affect the price or the approach, so flagging them in your first message means the fixed quote you get is the one that sticks.

Local Knowledge

Garden waste disposal in Flintshire: your options

Worth knowing before you decide how to tackle an overgrown garden — whether you use us or not.

The council brown bin in Flintshire is an opt-in, paid subscription — £38 per bin for 2026 — with fortnightly collections from March to the end of November and, new for 2026, monthly collections over winter. It's a well-run service for routine clippings: the county composts the lot at its Greenfield site and gives the finished compost back to residents free at the recycling centres. But the bin is a 140-litre one emptied fortnightly, and the council can't accept large branches, turf, soil or invasive plant species — which is most of what actually comes off a genuinely overgrown garden.

Flintshire's five household recycling centres — at Mold (Nercwys Road), Buckley (Globe Way), Sandycroft in Deeside, Rockliffe at Oakenholt and Greenfield — accept household garden waste, with proof of Flintshire residency required. Free to use, but you still do the cutting, the bagging and the repeated car trips yourself, and van and trailer visits need a permit.

Hiring a clearance service means the cutting, clearing, loading and disposal all happen in one visit for one fixed price. One legal point matters whoever you hire, and it applies in Wales exactly as it does in England: under the Environmental Protection Act 1990, householders have a duty of care for waste leaving their property, and you can be fined if it's later fly-tipped. Waste carrier registration is valid across England and Wales, so always ask for the trader's registration number and check it on the public register — we'll happily show you ours before we load a single bag.

Garden clearance in Flintshire — FAQs

How much does garden clearance cost in Flintshire?

It depends on the size of the garden and how much green waste needs removing, which is why every job is quoted individually. Send photos through the quote form and you'll receive a free fixed price, usually within one working day — and the price we agree is the price you pay.

Do you take the garden waste away?

Yes. Everything we cut and clear is loaded into the van on the day and, as a registered waste carrier, taken to a licensed waste site. No bags left out, no pile in the corner, no skip on the drive.

Can you clear a very overgrown or neglected garden?

Yes — overgrown gardens are our speciality. Brambles, knee-high grass, nettles and years of neglect: there's no judgement here. Many of the gardens we clear across Flintshire haven't been touched in years, and they're exactly the jobs we're set up for.

Do you work on both sides of the England–Wales border?

Yes. We're based locally and work daily across the border — Flintshire and Cheshire West & Chester are both core service areas. Waste carrier registration applies across England and Wales, so green waste from Welsh jobs is legally disposed of at a licensed site exactly as it is on the English side.

Do I need to be home during the clearance?

Not usually, as long as we can access the garden. Many Flintshire customers simply leave a gate unlocked and come home to a cleared garden and a set of before-and-after photos.

Which parts of Flintshire do you cover?

We cover the whole county, including Mold, Buckley, Deeside, Connah's Quay, Shotton, Queensferry, Hawarden, Ewloe, Flint, Holywell, Mynydd Isa, Northop, Broughton, Penyffordd, Hope and Bagillt — broadly the CH5, CH6, CH7 and CH8 postcodes plus the Flintshire parts of CH4. If your village isn't listed, ask — we can usually help.

How quickly can you clear a garden in Flintshire?

Most Flintshire gardens are cleared in a single day, and same-week slots are often available. Quotes are usually returned within one working day of receiving photos, so a garden can often go from first enquiry to fully cleared within a week.
